Grading Basketball Game

3 Pointer

This game is a metaphor to help you visualize some of the negative consequences of current grading systems. I wanted to focus on two of the main problems with grading: A decrease in interest in the subject and a decrease in risk-taking.

I came up with this metaphor based on the time I spent with my best friend playing Horse during open basketball in gym class. We were playing for fun and we weren't being judged based on our skills, so we had fun trying crazy trick shots and it was always very exciting when we managed to make one that was particulalry tricky. This concept is translated to the game by giving an option between going for a 2-pointer or a 3-pointer. At the start, when there is no grade, Many you may be inclined to go for the 3-pointer. You are just playing by yourself, so it's not like making a 3 will help you win anything, but it is more satisfying to make a difficult shot compared to the comparatively easy 2-pointer. This was meant to capture the idea of intrinsic motivation. You are practicing and trying to get better because you want to.

After this, you are in basketball class where your coach is grading students based on thier ability to make a basket. In this scenario, in order to satisfy the requirements, you just need to make a basket. Sure you could go for the riskier and more gratifying 3-pointer, but if you miss, well you've just failed the class. This demonstrates the risk-aversion that current grading systems create for students.

This is a fairly simple metaphor so it doesn't fully encompass all of the issues of current grading systems, but it does give a good overview. I would say one important thing This metaphor doens't do the best job demonstrating is the decrease in interest of the subject due to extrinsic motivation. I tried to represent this through the sad face on the student's face, but admittedly, the main thing this demonstration shows is the risk aversion factor.
